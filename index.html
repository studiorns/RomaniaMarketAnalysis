Market Insights

Key Takeaway

The Romania market shows a distinct autumn seasonality with peaks in October-November. Q4 represents the highest travel query period, accounting for over 40% of annual queries.

Seasonal Pattern

The Romania market demonstrates a distinct seasonal pattern with hotel guests peaking in August (4,724) and September (4,685), with October (4,209) as a close third. For travel queries, the highest peak occurs in October (4.56), followed by November (4.57). The lowest performance for both metrics occurs during the summer months (May-July).

Seasonal Distribution

  • Winter (Dec-Feb): 28% of annual travel queries
  • Spring (Mar-May): 18% of annual travel queries
  • Summer (Jun-Aug): 14% of annual travel queries
  • Fall (Sep-Nov): 40% of annual travel queries

Peak Performance

  • Top Month (Travel Queries): November (4.57) in 2024; November (8.41) projected for 2025
  • Top Month (Hotel Guests): August (4,724)
  • Top Month (Flight Searches): October (1,120)
  • Secondary Peak: January-February

Implications for Marketing

The distinct autumn seasonality of the Romania market suggests that marketing efforts should be concentrated in Q2 and early Q3 to capture the peak booking window for fall travel. The 67-day average booking window indicates that campaigns should be launched approximately 2 months before the target travel period.

Strategic Opportunity

The summer months (June-August) represent an opportunity for growth, as they currently account for only 14% of annual travel queries. Targeted summer campaigns highlighting Abu Dhabi's indoor attractions and luxury hotel experiences could help balance the seasonal distribution.

2025 Forecast Insight

The 2025 forecast shows a significant shift in peak travel query months to October-November across all scenarios, with November projected to reach 8.41 in the moderate scenario (an 84% increase from November 2024). This dramatic shift suggests a substantial evolution in the Romania market's seasonal pattern, with the peak period extending further into Q4 and showing much stronger performance.

Key Takeaway

The Romania market shows a concerning downward trend in intent (-0.36pp) despite increases in awareness (+3.12pp) and consideration (+3.45pp). This suggests challenges in converting interest into actual travel intent.

Market Performance

The Romania market has shown mixed performance in 2024. While awareness remains strong at 67.12% (+3.12pp vs Q4'23) and consideration has improved to 19.45% (+3.45pp), intent has declined to 6.64% (-0.36pp). This pattern suggests that while Abu Dhabi is becoming better known among Romanian travelers, there are barriers preventing consideration from converting to intent.

Positive Trends

  • Awareness: Increased by 3.12pp to 67.12%
  • Consideration: Increased by 3.45pp to 19.45%
  • Hotel Guests: 184.33% YoY growth in 2024
  • Flight Searches: 33.97% YoY growth in 2024
  • Travel Queries: 25.17% YoY growth in 2024

Negative Trends

  • Intent: Decreased by 0.36pp to 6.64%
  • Intent/Consideration Ratio: Decreased by 0.10 to 0.34
  • Summer Performance: Continued weakness in Jun-Aug

Market Challenges

The decline in intent metrics despite improved awareness and consideration suggests a need for renewed marketing approaches. With a strong growth forecast of 28.5% YoY in the moderate scenario, Romania requires targeted strategies that address the specific barriers identified in the survey data while building on the market's growing awareness foundation.

Market Challenge

The Romania market shows a concerning Intent/Consideration ratio of 0.34, which is among the lowest in all markets. This indicates that while Romanians are increasingly considering Abu Dhabi as a destination, they are not converting to actual travel intent at the expected rate. Addressing the key barriers of restrictive laws/customs perception (52%), unfriendly people perception (36%), and safety concerns (31%) could significantly improve this conversion rate.

Key Takeaway

Strategic recommendations for the Romania market focus on addressing perceptions of restrictive laws/customs, improving the perception of local hospitality, enhancing safety perception, addressing the lack of direct flights, and developing targeted campaigns for the peak autumn season.

Address Restrictive Laws/Customs Perception

Implement a "Modern Abu Dhabi" campaign to counter the high perception of restrictive laws/customs (52%).

  • Create content highlighting the cosmopolitan lifestyle and modern aspects of Abu Dhabi
  • Develop a Romanian-language guide to Abu Dhabi customs and etiquette
  • Partner with Romanian influencers to showcase the ease of visiting
  • Feature testimonials from Romanian visitors about their experiences

Projected outcome: Reduce "restrictive laws/customs" perception by 8-10pp

Hospitality Perception Enhancement

Launch a "Warm Welcome" campaign to address the increasing perception that people are not friendly/helpful (36%, +4pp).

  • Create content featuring local hospitality workers and their stories
  • Develop a series of videos showcasing positive interactions between locals and Romanian tourists
  • Partner with Romanian travel agencies to provide cultural briefings
  • Implement a Romanian language welcome program at key tourist sites

Projected outcome: Reduce "unfriendly people" perception by 6-8pp

Direct Flight Initiative

Address the growing concern about lack of direct flights (18%, +7pp) through strategic airline partnerships.

  • Engage with airlines to explore direct flight options from Bucharest to Abu Dhabi
  • Develop special packages with connecting flights through major European hubs
  • Create content highlighting the ease of reaching Abu Dhabi with one-stop connections
  • Partner with travel agencies to offer streamlined booking processes

Projected outcome: Mitigate the "no direct flights" barrier through improved connectivity options

Seasonal Campaign Strategy

Develop a "Perfect Autumn in Abu Dhabi" campaign to capitalize on the strong Q4 seasonality.

  • Create content highlighting Abu Dhabi's ideal climate during October-November
  • Develop special autumn packages with Romanian travel partners
  • Implement targeted media campaigns in August-September (accounting for the 2-month lag)
  • Feature autumn events and activities that appeal to Romanian travelers

Projected outcome: Increase Q4 travel queries by 30-35% YoY
